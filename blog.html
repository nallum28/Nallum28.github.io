Reflecting on My Honours Project: Detecting and Preventing Evil Twin Attacks

As I submitted my honours project and dissertation, I found myself reflecting on the journey it took to develop a comprehensive tool aimed at detecting and preventing Evil Twin attacks. This project was not only a significant academic milestone but also a real-world challenge in the cybersecurity space, highlighting the critical need for better wireless network security.

The Challenge of Evil Twin Attacks

Evil Twin attacks are a form of Wi-Fi phishing that exploit the way we connect to wireless networks. In these attacks, a malicious actor sets up a rogue access point that mimics a legitimate one, tricking users into connecting to it. Once connected, attackers can intercept sensitive data, inject malicious payloads, or simply disrupt the connection. The sophistication of these attacks made them an intriguing topic for my honours project.

The problem, however, was clear: detecting and preventing Evil Twin attacks required a tool that could not only identify rogue access points but also actively defend against them. That’s where my project came in.

Creating the Tool

The project aimed to build a practical, deployable solution on Kali Linux, a distribution that is widely used for penetration testing and security research. The tool I created combined several powerful open-source libraries and tools to perform a variety of functions:

  • Aircrack-ng: I used Aircrack-ng for monitoring Wi-Fi traffic and identifying access points. It helped capture packets and detect rogue APs (Access Points) by analyzing network behavior and SSIDs.
  • Scapy: Scapy, a powerful Python-based tool, was invaluable for packet crafting and manipulation. I leveraged it to create customized packets that could inject into the network to test whether a specific AP was attempting to impersonate a legitimate one.
  • WPA Handshake Cracking: The tool also had the ability to attempt WPA handshake cracking on the captured data, further verifying the legitimacy of the access point and ensuring no rogue entities could bypass security measures.

Developing the Dissertation

While the tool was being built, I simultaneously worked on my dissertation, which provided a comprehensive analysis of the problem, the techniques used to detect Evil Twin attacks, and the methods for preventing them. The dissertation was both a technical breakdown and an exploration of the wider implications of wireless network security.

I examined several detection strategies, from simple passive scanning (looking for discrepancies in network behavior) to more active detection methods (forcing probes and detecting the rogue AP’s response). In terms of prevention, I focused on techniques such as device whitelisting, certificate-based authentication, and other mechanisms that could be incorporated into existing network security protocols.

The dissertation also explored real-world scenarios, considering both home and enterprise-level Wi-Fi networks, as well as the evolving threats posed by more sophisticated actors using multiple rogue APs to create man-in-the-middle scenarios.

Lessons Learned

Reflecting on this experience, I realize that the project was a balancing act of theory, practical application, and learning to adapt tools in creative ways. While theoretical knowledge was essential for understanding the mechanics of Evil Twin attacks, the actual tool development pushed me to understand the limits of existing tools and how to integrate them into a cohesive system.

One of the most valuable lessons I took away was the importance of real-world testing. Although simulations are useful, I found that testing in live environments provided insights that were impossible to predict in theory. The unpredictability of real-world networks added layers of complexity to the tool’s design, and overcoming these hurdles taught me how to think critically under pressure.

Looking Ahead: The Future of Wireless Security

After the submission, I reflected on how Evil Twin attacks continue to evolve and how my tool could potentially be expanded. As Wi-Fi networks become more advanced and ubiquitous, the need for innovative security measures will only grow. I hope that this project serves as a stepping stone for future work in wireless security—whether that’s through machine learning-based detection or AI-driven mitigation strategies that could detect patterns of behavior that human analysis might miss.

Ultimately, this honours project has deepened my passion for network security, and I am excited to continue exploring the many challenges that lie ahead. It’s a reminder of how vital it is to stay ahead of the curve and always be one step ahead of attackers. I am grateful for the opportunity to have worked on something that could contribute to a safer digital future.

Hosting a Cybersecurity Conference at the University: Reflections from the Treasurer

One of the most exciting and rewarding experiences I had as the treasurer for the Ethical Hacking Club was hosting a Cybersecurity Conference at the university. This event brought together students, industry experts, and academics to discuss the latest trends, challenges, and innovations in cybersecurity. From financial planning to event execution, it was an opportunity to get hands-on experience in organizing a large-scale event while diving deep into the cybersecurity world.

Planning and Organizing the Event

As the treasurer, my role was critical in managing the conference’s budget and ensuring that everything ran smoothly from a financial standpoint. This meant coordinating with vendors, securing sponsorships, and managing the funds to cover the costs of speakers, food, and materials for the attendees. The process was more complex than I initially anticipated, but it was also a learning experience that helped me develop valuable event management skills.

One of the first steps was to work closely with the rest of the committee. After securing the venue by meeting with the students association and university security, we reached out to potential sponsors and vendors to ensure we had the financial backing and materials needed for the event.

Engaging Industry Experts and Speakers

One of the most exciting parts of the conference was the opportunity to bring in industry experts from various fields within cybersecurity. We had speakers from both the public and private sectors, including ethical hackers, penetration testers, and network security specialists. Their insights into real-world security challenges, evolving threats, and emerging technologies were invaluable to the students in attendance.

In addition to the main talks, we also held hands-on workshops, where students could learn practical cybersecurity skills, such as how to conduct vulnerability assessments, use penetration testing tools, and protect networks from attacks. These interactive sessions allowed attendees to gain real-world experience and skills that could be directly applied in their future careers.

Managing the Finances: A Key Responsibility

Managing the budget was undoubtedly one of the most challenging aspects of organizing the event. I had to ensure that the conference remained financially viable while still offering a high-quality experience for all attendees. This involved tracking expenses, coordinating payments for venue rental, catering, speaker fees, and promotional materials, while also ensuring that we stayed within our allocated budget.

Throughout the event, I had to ensure all payments were processed promptly and that we had enough funds for any unexpected costs. I also had to work with the other members of the Ethical Hacking Club to keep track of sponsorships and ticket sales, ensuring that everything was accounted for and that no detail was overlooked.

The Impact on Students and the University Community

The most rewarding part of hosting the conference was seeing how it impacted the students and the wider university community. The event provided an opportunity for students to network with professionals, explore internship opportunities, and gain valuable insights into the cybersecurity industry. It also gave them a chance to interact with like-minded peers who shared their passion for security.

As the treasurer, it was incredibly fulfilling to see the event come together successfully. The feedback we received from attendees, speakers, and sponsors was overwhelmingly positive, and it was clear that the conference made a significant impact. It was also a reminder of how important it is for universities to provide opportunities like this for students to learn, grow, and connect with industry professionals.

Lessons Learned and Future Plans

Hosting the cybersecurity conference was a huge learning experience. Not only did I learn about financial management, event planning, and coordination, but I also learned a great deal about the challenges and opportunities within the cybersecurity field. It was fascinating to hear from professionals who had firsthand experience with cutting-edge technologies, and it inspired me to pursue my own career in cybersecurity even more.

Looking back, I’m proud of what we achieved, and I’m grateful for the opportunity to contribute to the success of the event. I also hope that future conferences will continue to build on what we started and provide even more opportunities for students to engage with the cybersecurity community.

The conference also sparked new ideas for future events, including workshops, seminars, and collaborative projects with other universities. As the cybersecurity landscape continues to evolve, it’s essential that we continue to provide students with opportunities to learn, connect, and stay ahead of the curve.

Immersive Campus Day

The Cybersecurity Room: A Digital Investigation Unfolds

The event revolved around a murder mystery with five suspects, each with potential motives and hidden connections to the victim. In my room, students became part of the digital investigation. I projected my screen to the room, demonstrating OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence) techniques and password cracking in real time.

While I led the technical investigation, the students weren’t just passive observers—they interacted at various points, contributing ideas, making observations, and helping to piece together the clues. Their input was key as we uncovered private messages between the suspect and victim, providing critical evidence for the courtroom portion of the day.

From Concept to Execution

Creating this experience required close collaboration with the overall lead and the scriptwriter responsible for the day’s narrative. We worked together to ensure that the cybersecurity portion fit seamlessly into the storyline, while keeping the techniques realistic but accessible for the 14-16 year old audience. The goal was to make it educational, engaging, and slightly cinematic—giving students a glimpse into how digital investigations are conducted.

Impact and Reflections

Watching the students engage with the cybersecurity process—from spotting key details to reacting to the unfolding evidence—was incredibly rewarding. Their curiosity and willingness to participate highlighted just how impactful hands-on, immersive learning can be.

Returning to the outreach office afterward, I reflected on how this event came together so quickly after the school visit. It was a reminder of how spontaneous opportunities can lead to some of the most memorable experiences. The chance to showcase cybersecurity skills in an interactive format, while collaborating with creative leads, was a highlight—and hopefully, it sparked some interest in tech and digital forensics among the students.

Hosting Campus Tours for Prospective Students: A Look into the Cybersecurity and Networking Labs

As a computing student and mentor, one of my favorite opportunities was hosting campus tours for prospective students, giving them an inside look at what university life was really like. These tours weren’t just about walking through campus; they were about offering a glimpse into the world of computing and technology, showing how the university prepared students for real-world careers in fields like cybersecurity and computer networks.

The Excitement of Welcoming Future Cybersecurity Experts

When prospective students arrived, they were often eager to learn about the latest technologies, the campus environment, and the hands-on learning opportunities available to them. As we made our way through the campus, one of the first stops was always the Cybersecurity Lab. This state-of-the-art facility was where students like me explored everything from ethical hacking to security analysis, network protection, and real-time threat monitoring.

During the tour, I took time to highlight some of the key equipment and software we used to prepare for cybersecurity challenges. From firewalls and intrusion detection systems to advanced penetration testing tools, the lab was equipped to simulate real-world scenarios where students could safely learn the ins and outs of digital security. It was incredible to see prospective students light up when they realized just how much they could accomplish in this space!

The Networking Labs: Building the Future of Connectivity

Next, we headed over to the Networking Lab, where the magic of computer networking came to life. Here, students practiced setting up, configuring, and troubleshooting networks that spanned local, wide, and global distances. From understanding the fundamental concepts of IP addressing and routing protocols to building complex, enterprise-level networks, the lab was an essential part of the learning process for any aspiring network engineer.

As we walked through the lab, I shared some of the experiences I had working on networking projects, from setting up multi-layered networks to experimenting with wireless protocols and security measures. The real-world applications of the skills learned in this lab were vast, and many students were surprised by how closely the lab mirrored industry-standard practices.

Why These Labs Mattered

For prospective students, the Cybersecurity and Networking Labs were more than just classrooms — they were immersive environments that provided real-world experience. These labs were central to the university's approach to teaching and were key to preparing students for careers in tech. By showcasing these spaces, I hoped to give future students an idea of the level of expertise and hands-on training they could expect when they stepped onto campus.

It was inspiring to see prospective students become more confident and excited about their future in tech as they explored the possibilities these labs offered. I often heard comments like, “This is exactly what I want to be doing!” or “I didn’t know these kinds of labs even existed!” It was always rewarding to witness their curiosity sparked by the cutting-edge technology and the chance to learn from experts in the field.

Looking Ahead: Shaping the Next Generation of Cybersecurity and Network Professionals

Hosting these campus tours was a fulfilling experience for me because it was a chance to share my journey, the skills I developed, and the exciting future that lay ahead in fields like cybersecurity and networking. As we concluded the tour, I encouraged prospective students to take advantage of all the opportunities offered there — to learn, grow, and build their careers in one of the most dynamic industries today.

If you’re thinking about a career in tech, I highly recommend exploring what these labs had to offer. The university’s investment in top-tier labs and real-world learning experiences was something that set it apart from other institutions. The future of cybersecurity and networking was bright, and I was proud to be a part of it.

Reflecting on My First Shift as a Football Steward at Motherwell FC: Dealing with Away Fans

One of the most unexpected yet rewarding experiences I had during my time volunteering was my first shift as a football steward at Motherwell FC. While my main focus was on cybersecurity and tech, this opportunity provided me with a unique chance to step into a different environment and develop new skills. Managing crowds, ensuring safety, and dealing with the challenges that come with large events like football matches, especially with away fans, was a learning experience that taught me a lot about leadership, responsibility, and communication.

The Role of a Steward

As a steward, my primary responsibilities included ensuring that the crowd behaved appropriately, helping fans find their seats, checking tickets, and being available in case of emergencies. The role was far more interactive and people-centric than I had initially expected, but it gave me the chance to hone my problem-solving skills on the spot. It also required me to remain calm under pressure and manage situations where emotions ran high.

One of the most important aspects of the role was dealing with the away fans. Football can be an emotional game, and tensions often run high, particularly when the away team is involved. My shift required me to balance keeping the peace with ensuring that fans were able to enjoy the match in a safe environment. I quickly realized that good communication and maintaining a calm and authoritative presence were key to diffusing any potential issues before they escalated.

Managing the Away Fans

As it was my first shift, I was placed in the section where the away fans were seated. This presented unique challenges, as fans of the away team can sometimes be more passionate and vocal, especially when things aren’t going well for their team. However, I learned that most incidents could be avoided by staying on top of things, being proactive, and showing empathy for the fans.

Throughout the game, I had to monitor the situation closely, ensuring that the fans didn’t get too rowdy or disruptive. At one point, a group of away supporters began chanting aggressively, which could have led to tensions with the home fans. Instead of allowing things to escalate, I stepped in, spoke with the group, and explained the need for maintaining a respectful environment for everyone. Luckily, they responded positively and calmed down, and the situation was resolved without incident. This was a perfect example of how clear communication and empathy can help defuse potential conflicts.

Lessons Learned: Communication and Leadership

Reflecting on that first shift, I realized how much I had learned in a short amount of time. The key takeaway for me was the importance of communication and leadership in a high-pressure environment. Whether it was dealing with a difficult situation or just ensuring that fans were comfortable, my ability to remain calm, assertive, and approachable made all the difference. It also showed me how much effective teamwork matters, as we were all working together to keep the event running smoothly.

Additionally, this experience helped me understand how important it is to be adaptable. Every shift is different, and you never know when a new challenge will arise. From managing crowd control to helping fans with issues, I was constantly having to think on my feet and make quick decisions that would ensure the safety and enjoyment of everyone involved.

Reflections: A Valuable Experience

Overall, my first shift as a steward at Motherwell FC was a valuable experience. While it was challenging, it was also incredibly rewarding. I gained new insights into dealing with crowds, managing difficult situations, and providing exceptional customer service. It also gave me a greater appreciation for the behind-the-scenes work that goes into ensuring that large events like football matches run smoothly.

As I begin to build my career in cybersecurity, I know that the skills I gained from this experience—such as communication, problem-solving, and conflict resolution—will serve me well in any field. It reminded me that no matter what industry you’re in, these fundamental skills are essential for success. And while I may have been outside of my usual comfort zone, it reinforced the idea that stepping outside of it can lead to tremendous personal growth.
